Who is Risa?

Risa worked for The Difference Engine as a Web Designer and Developer where she consulted and worked on rebuilding websites for non-profit businesses. She has extensive experience as a graphic designer and patent illustration drafter working with companies like Verizon Wireless, Argonne National Laboratory, Reckless Records, and many more. She was formerly a Computer Arts teacher for Yollocalli Arts Reach through After School Matters and has been hired as a professional Japanese-English translator.

Risa holds an IB from Seisen International School in Tokyo, Japan and a BFA in Visual Communications/Art & Technology Studies from The School of the Art Institute of Chicago. She is a 2001 James Raymond Nelson Fellowship recipient and was awarded a Merit Scholarship upon enrollment to the school. She also studied, toured, and worked with a Chicago-based collaborative, Goat Island Performance Group. Last year, she completed a full-stack web developer program with Actualize and was hired immediately after graduation. She has been a Teaching Assistant for Actualizeâ€™s Full Stack Web Developer Program for two cohorts and believes in open source learning.

She takes studying seriously and her favorite sport is table tennis. She is curious, full of questions, dabbles in many creative coding projects as possible, and and loves to build dioramas. Risa recently graduated from an intensive six-month Data Science Bootcamp at Northwestern University. In her spare time, she serves as a volunteer for Open Books, supports youth STEM education, and belongs to The Japan America Society of Chicago network.
